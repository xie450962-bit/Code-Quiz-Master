## Brand & Style

The design system is rooted in a professional, corporate identity that balances Japanese precision with global modernism. The brand personality is authoritative yet energetic, designed to evoke feelings of reliability, strategic insight, and forward momentum.

The design style is **Corporate Modern**. It utilizes a clean, high-contrast aesthetic with generous whitespace to ensure clarity. It avoids excessive decorative flourishes, instead relying on deliberate color accents and a structured layout to communicate stability and expertise. The visual language is functional and systematic, ensuring that complex information is accessible and trustworthy.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by a high-energy **Primary Red (#F13A3C)**, used strategically for calls to action, highlights, and brand markers to signal dynamism. 

**Secondary Charcoal (#333333)** serves as the primary typographic color and structural element, providing a grounded, professional contrast. The **Neutral Off-White (#F8F9FA)** is used for section backgrounds to provide subtle tonal separation from the **Pure White (#FFFFFF)** base. This hierarchy ensures that the interface feels airy but structured, with a clear focus on content legibility.

## Typography

This design system uses a dual-typeface approach to bridge international corporate standards with high legibility. 

**Montserrat** is the display face, used for headlines and labels. Its geometric structure provides a modern, confident tone. **Noto Sans** (replacing Yu Gothic for digital-first consistency) is used for all body text to ensure maximum readability across platforms and languages, particularly for dense corporate information. 

Large headlines should use tighter letter spacing to maintain a cohesive visual block, while labels and small caps can benefit from increased tracking for a premium feel.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout follows a **Fixed Grid** model on desktop to maintain an authoritative, centered presence. It utilizes a 12-column system with a 24px gutter. 

Spacing is based on an 8px modular scale to ensure mathematical harmony between elements. 
- **Desktop:** 1200px max-width container. Section vertical padding is generous (80px - 120px) to allow content to breathe.
- **Tablet:** 8-column grid with 32px side margins.
- **Mobile:** Single-column fluid layout with 16px side margins. Typography scales down specifically for the mobile viewport to prevent excessive wrapping.

## Elevation & Depth

This design system prioritizes a flat, structural hierarchy rather than deep shadows. Depth is achieved through:

1.  **Tonal Layers:** Using the neutral `#F8F9FA` against white backgrounds to define content zones.
2.  **Low-Contrast Outlines:** Subtle 1px borders in a light gray are used for cards and input fields rather than heavy shadows.
3.  **Flat Overlays:** For hover states or modals, a solid color shift or a very soft, diffused ambient shadow (0px 4px 20px, 5% opacity black) is used to maintain the clean, "un-cluttered" professional aesthetic.

## Shapes

The shape language is "Soft," utilizing minimal 4px (`0.25rem`) corner radii. This maintains a sharp, precise corporate feel while avoiding the clinical harshness of perfectly square corners. Interactive elements like buttons and input fields follow this standard, while decorative icons or secondary accents may use the slightly larger `rounded-lg` (8px) for subtle differentiation.

## Components

### Buttons
- **Primary:** Solid `#F13A3C` background with white text. No shadow, 4px rounded corners. Transition to a darker red on hover.
- **Secondary:** Transparent background with a 1px `#333333` border and charcoal text.
- **Tertiary:** Text-only with a red underline that expands on hover.

### Input Fields
- White background with a 1px light gray border. On focus, the border transitions to Primary Red. Labels are small-cap Montserrat for a refined look.

### Cards
- No shadows. Use a subtle `#F8F9FA` background or a 1px border. Content inside should have 32px internal padding to maintain the "breathable" corporate aesthetic.

### Chips & Tags
- Rectangular with 2px roundedness. Used for categories or status indicators. High contrast background for active states; neutral light gray for inactive.

### Lists
- Use the Primary Red for bullet points or custom icons to tie the lists back to the brand identity. Spacing between list items should be at least 12px to ensure readability.
